Tips for an Objective

When creating an Objective. You want to create something that is "Completable" in the sense that there is a defined end that you have in mind.

For instance, a defined goal could be to "lose 10 pounds". This means that by the end of all of your progress tracking will weigh 10 pounds less than the time you set the goal.

You can also set more interpretable goals like "Hold a Conversation in Spanish". As long as you can clearly articulate the end result and how to measure it, the goal can be set up this way.

Tips for Subobjectives

When creating a subobjective, define something that will help you achieve your overall goal and is measurable by actions. For example if your objective was to "lose 10 pounds", then one of your subobjectives could be "Excercise for 30 Minutes".

Subobjectives are the driving mechanism for achieving your objectives, so make sure that you get specific on how you define them and make them achievable. It is much better to break you subobjectives to be more granular if necessary (i.e. make one subojective "Cardio for atleast 20 Minutes" and another be "Lift Weights for atleast 20 minutes".
